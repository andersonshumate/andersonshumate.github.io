The Late-Night Monologue

The monologue is, technically speaking, the first ten or so minutes, usually a series of jokes, that opens a late-night talk show. By nature, the monologue is observational.

But what makes a monologue unique? A memorable joke? The delivery by the host? The socio-political context? The fact that the host stumbled walking to their desk?

When The Tonight Show premiered locally in New York in 1954, television broadcasting by NBC was only ___ years old.

The monologue, as we know it today, was well into form by the Jack Paar years starting in 1957.
